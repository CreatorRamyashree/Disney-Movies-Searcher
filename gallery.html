The History of Movies

Movies began in the late 1800s with silent films and early motion experiments. Sound was introduced in the 1920s, transforming cinema. The mid-1900s saw Hollywood’s golden age, global genres, and technological innovations like color and widescreen. Blockbusters like "Star Wars" emerged in the 1970s. The digital era (1990s–present) brought CGI, streaming, and global filmmaking. Movies continue to evolve with AI and virtual production.

The History of Disney

Disney was founded in 1923 by Walt and Roy Disney as a small animation studio. It gained fame with Mickey Mouse in 1928 and the first feature-length animated film, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937). The company expanded into theme parks with Disneyland (1955) and later Walt Disney World (1971).
In the 1990s, Disney acquired Pixar, Marvel, Lucasfilm (Star Wars), and 21st Century Fox, solidifying its dominance in entertainment. Today, it is a global powerhouse in animation, live-action films, streaming (Disney+), and theme parks, renowned for its storytelling and iconic characters.
